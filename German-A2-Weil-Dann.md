Understanding the difference between **weil** (because) and **dann** (then) is a huge step in mastering German sentence structure. The most important thing to remember is that German is obsessed with where the **verb** goes!

---

## 1. Weil (Because)
**Weil** is a subordinating conjunction. This means it kicks the conjugated verb all the way to the **very end** of the clause.

* **Rule:** [Main Clause], **weil** + [Subject] + [Other Info] + **Verb**.
* **Context:** Use it to give a reason or explanation.

### Examples:
| German Sentence | English Meaning |
| :--- | :--- |
| Ich bleibe zu Hause, **weil** ich krank **bin**. | I am staying at home **because** I **am** sick. |
| Er lernt Deutsch, **weil** er in Berlin arbeiten **will**. | He is learning German **because** he **wants** to work in Berlin. |
| Wir essen Pizza, **weil** wir Hunger **haben**. | We are eating pizza **because** we **are** hungry (have hunger). |

---

## 2. Dann (Then / After that)
**Dann** is an adverb used to describe a sequence of events. In German, the verb must always be the **second element** in a statement. If you start a sentence with *dann*, the verb comes immediately after it.

* **Rule:** **Dann** + **Verb** + [Subject] + [Other Info].
* **Context:** Use it to say what happens next in a timeline.

### Examples:
| German Sentence | English Meaning |
| :--- | :--- |
| Zuerst dusche ich, **dann** **frühstücke** ich. | First I shower, **then** I **eat breakfast**. |
| Wir gehen ins Kino und **dann** **trinken** wir ein Bier. | We are going to the cinema and **then** we **are drinking** a beer. |
| Erst machen wir Hausaufgaben, **dann** **spielen** wir. | First we do homework, **then** we **play**. |

---

## Summary Cheat Sheet

* **Weil** = **Verb at the end.** It answers "Why?"
* **Dann** = **Verb in position 2.** It answers "When next?"

> **Pro Tip:** A common mistake is saying *"weil ich bin krank."* Even though it sounds right to an English speaker, a German ear is waiting for that verb to land at the end like a plane on a runway: *"weil ich krank **bin**."*
